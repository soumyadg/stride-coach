## What the market teaches us
- **TrainAsONE** already beats the leading app on the two things the leading app is weakest at: **daily adaptation** + **missed-workout restructuring**. But its UX is clunky and it's niche.
- **Nike/adidas** win on "free + polished" but lose on real personalization.
- **The gap nobody owns:** *polished UX (Nike-level)* + *genuinely adaptive & injury-safe (better than TrainAsONE)* + *honest free tier (unlike the leading app)*. **That's our lane.**

## The injury wedge (our headline differentiator)
the leading app's structural flaw (the5krunner, TechRadar, PT reports, 2M users):
1. Algorithm **"takes the runner at their word"** — beginners self-assess badly → over-prescribed.
2. **No mid-cycle feedback loop** — can't detect a struggling athlete without manual input.
3. **Ignores wearable data** for overtraining detection (Strava owns the data, doesn't use it here).
4. Ramps load faster than a human coach would moderate → stress fractures, shin splints, Achilles tendinopathy.
5. the leading app is now *reactively* bolting on "dial back intensity" — validates the problem.

**We build injury-safety in from day one**, not as a patch.
